     Transaction
     Data
Transaction data reflect the
consequences of executing process steps,
or transactions. Examples of transaction
data are dates, quantities, prices, and
payment and delivery terms. Thus,
transaction data are a combination of
organizational data, master data, and
situational data— that is, data that are
specific to the task being executed, such
as who, what, when, and where.
