Professor : PhD. (C) Luz A. Adanaqué
I’m an Electronics Engineer from the National University of Engineering with a
Master’s in Embedded Systems for Data Processing from the University of
Bologna, and I’m currently pursuing a PhD in Computer Science at PUCP.

Over the past decade, I’ve worked at INICTEL-UNI in the Radio Frequency,
Microwave, and Optics Group, leading Peru’s participation in APSCO projects and
contributing to cutting-edge research in circuits and systems.

I’ve also been actively involved in undergraduate teaching and participation as
an IEEE student branch advisor. My academic and professional interests span
human–computer        interaction,   technology   acceptance    models,     and
high-performance computing, my doctoral research focused on fast, parallel, and
scalable algorithms for 4K image ﬁltering.
